Nicholas Taylor snips a play

Hyacinth Halvey by Lady Agusta Gregory

MRS. DELANE.

To-day it is he is coming, and it's likely he was in that train. There was a card about him to Sergeant Carden this morning.

MR. QUIRKE.

A young chap from Carrow they were saying he was.

MRS. DELANE.

So he is, one Hyacinth Halvey; and indeed if all that is said of him is true, or if a quarter of it is true, he will be a credit to this town.

MR. QUIRKE.

Is that so?

MRS. DELANE.

Testimonials he has by the score. To Father Gregan they were sent. Registered they were coming and going. Would you believe me telling you that they weighed up to three pounds?

MR. QUIRKE.

There must be great bulk in them indeed.

MRS. DELANE.

It is no wonder he to get the job. He must have a great character, so many persons to write for him as what there did.
